Setting Up Bone LOD

Bone LOD is a special feature of the Gamebryo animation system to optimize skinned characters. The Bone LOD system allows you to select the different bones of a character and mark them for a particular level of detail. The focus of the system is to allow you to automatically generate simpler skeletons to use at lower levels of detail. For example, during in-engine cut scenes where the character's face is prominent, you want to use the highest level of detail and animate the character's expressions with facial bones. During regular game play, you want to use a simpler skeleton that doesn't use face bones to save the performance. Finally, when the character is 200 yards away and 30 pixels high you want to use a primitive skeleton that doesn't even have fingers.

Set Up Bone LOD

Look in the Maya Artist Examples directory for examples of Gamebryo LOD in Maya. In particular, Geometry/BoneLOD.mb deals with setting up Bone LOD.

To set up Bone LOD

  1. Load your skinned character in Maya.
  2. Select your character's Animation Root and click the Bone LOD button on the Gamebryo Shelf.
  3. You will now see the Gamebryo Bone LOD dialog as well as Maya's Hypergraph.
  4. Set the "Current Bone LOD" to 0 and click the Select Level button. You will see Maya's selection set change to show all the leaf objects. It is important to remember that the numbering schemes for Bone LOD levels are backwards. Bone LOD with the highest level of detail has the lowest number.
  5. Increase the Bone LOD level by one and click on the Select Level button.
  6. In the Hypergraph, select the lowest-level objects that should be part of this Bone LOD level. In the BoneLOD.mb example, you can see the simplification of the scene graph to remove the influence of low level bones that control the face.
  7. Keep repeating steps 5 and 6 until you are out of levels you want to create. In the BoneLOD.mb example, you can see that level 2 finally reduces the head to just a single joint. This is as simple as the character can get, but from a distance you can't see the facial expressions.

  8. Setup the character's Skin LOD.
