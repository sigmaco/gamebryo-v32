Gamebryo Lights

Lights in Gamebryo are fundamentally different than in Maya.  To give additional control to the artist, these attributes can be adjusted using "Extra Attributes" attached to the Light's shape.  These attributes are added through a button in the attribute editor for the lights.

General Lighting in the Gamebryo Maya Plug-in

Lights in Gamebryo have four major components:  dimmer, ambient color, diffuse color, and specular color.  Dimmer will scale the overall brightness of each light component.  The different color components affect their respective components within a material.  Negative intensity values are possible within Maya, but are considered 0.0 by the Gamebryo Maya Plug-in.  Spot lights in Gamebryo support both the "Cone Angle" and the "Penumbra Angle". Ambient lights are considered a special case in Gamebryo.  They only have an ambient color and will only affect the ambient color of a material, not its attenuation, as in Maya.

Ambient Lights in the Gamebryo Maya Plug-in

Gamebryo ambient lights only affect the ambient color of a material.

When rendering in Maya, you may notice that (a) the ambient light isn't strictly ambient (It has a point light component.), and (b) the ambient light affects both the diffuse and ambient components of each material.

When rendering in the Gamebryo Maya Plug-in, you will also notice that (a) the ambient light is strictly ambient and has no point light component, and (b) the ambient light only affects the ambient component of a material. The color of a textured object depends on the "Default Ambient Color for a Texture's Material" setting.
