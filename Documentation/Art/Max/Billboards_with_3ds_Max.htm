Billboards with 3ds Max

About Billboards

Billboards are a special type of object which the Gamebryo engine automatically transforms to face the camera. In many cases, good use of billboards can significantly optimize a scene by representing complex objects as simple quadrilaterals with textures that look like the object. Billboards can also simulate some effects that are otherwise difficult to achieve, such as volumetric lighting. There are several methods the engine can use to rotate the object, each of which is useful in different situations. These methods are described below.

Creating a billboard object in 3ds Max

To make a billboard object in Max, take the following steps:

  • Bring up the Billboard Helper Utility.
  • Select a type of billboard from the panel.
  • Select an object from the Max scene that you would like to represent as a billboard.
  • Press the "Apply to Selection" button on the panel. This adds a string to the object's user-defined properties that tells the exporter to set the appropriate engine flags.

It is important to note that Max does not support billboards as Gamebryo does, so when you apply this property, the object will not look or behave any differently in Max. To see the effects of the billboard property, launch the preview tool.

Axis alignment for billboards in 3ds Max

When an object is flagged as a billboard, Gamebryo knows to automatically align the object to face the camera. However, Gamebryo does not automatically know which side of the object the artist wants to face the camera, as well as which part of the of the object should be facing up. To simplify setting these values, the exporter assumes that the object's positive Y axis always faces up, and the object's local positive Z axis should be used to determine the billboard's orientation with respect to the camera.

Due to these assumptions, some adjustment may be needed for your billboards to look as expected in the engine. This can be solved easily by taking the following steps:

  • Select the billboard object
  • Click on the Hierarchy tab of the Max Command Panel
  • Under the "Adjust Pivot" rollout, click the "Affect Pivot Only" button.
  • You can now rotate the pivot as you would any Max object, but your rotations will not affect the object to which the pivot belongs.
  • Rotate the pivot until its positive Y axis is pointing in the "up" direction relative to the object, and the positive Z axis is pointing in the direction you want the camera to always "see" when viewing the object.
  • Uncheck the "Affect Pivot Only" button.

Types of billboards

Max supports five types of billboards. Each behaves differently as detailed below.

  • Standard—A Standard billboard's "forward" direction (local positive Z axis) will always face towards the camera. The billboard's "up" direction (local positive Y axis) will point in the direction closest to the world's up direction as it can while staying in the camera's view plane
  • Up—An Up billboard will always keep its "up" direction (the local positive Y axis) parallel to the world's up direction. It rotates about its up direction as needed to always have its "forward" direction (the local positive Z axis) face the camera. This is useful for objects like a tree in the distance because the tree will always display its full width, but when you go above the tree and look down, it won't appear that the tree is laying on the ground.
  • Rigid—A Rigid billboard always keeps its up direction parallel to the up direction of the camera, and it always keeps its forward direction parallel to the camera direction. This means that the object will act like a screen texture that gets larger and smaller based on its distance to the camera. This type of billboard could be used for a lighting effect such as a lens flare or a circular glow texture to represent volumetric lighting.
  • Center—A Center billboard is similar to a Standard billboard, but the Center billboard faces in the direction of the difference between the camera's location and the billboard's location. The advantage is that the billboard still faces the camera when the billboard is not centered on the screen.
  • Rigid Center—The Rigid Center billboard is an improved version of Rigid. It keeps its up direction parallel to the up direction of the camera, and it rotates as necessary to keep its forward direction parallel to the difference between the billboard location and the camera's location.
