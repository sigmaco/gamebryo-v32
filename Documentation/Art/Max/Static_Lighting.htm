Static Lighting

There are many ways to optimize your lights so it looks like you have many lights in your scene when you only have one or two real-dynamic lights. This technique is called Static lighting. You "burn" lighting into your geometry on a vertex or pixel level ahead of time, giving it the appearance of being lit. This technique is how most engines get such nice lighting while running at a decent frame rate. Static lighting can be done on a Vertex level by painting the vertices or on a pixel level when you need very detailed shadows by assigning Dark or Light Maps to your materials.

Shading with Vertex Colors

Using the Vertex paint modifier or the Assign Vertex Colors utility for vertex painting. To see the object in the max view port you need to check the 'Show vertex colors' flag in the User Defined Properties dialog of the object. The Gamebryo Shader must have the Vertex Color flag set on. The following modes will affect how this vertex lighting interacts with the environment:

  • If the Src Mode is Amb-Dif, then the vertex colors will be modulated onto your textures. This modulation will make them darker only, since white represents the original texture value. Dynamic lights (3ds max lights) in your scene will still affect them.
  • If the Src Mode is Emissive, the vertex colors are 'self-illuminating' your textures. They are statically lit by the vertex colors. This is the mode you would use for a completely static scene. If they are included in a light list, the vertex colors will be additive to the light color and will eventually wash out to white. Creating this mode in Max is tricky, as Max likes to include lights.
  • The third mode, Ignore should not be used for lighting, as it is for using the vertex colors for non-visual data.

Shading with Dark Maps

To create Dark-Maps for intricate shadows, you must render the shadows from a view port that matches how you want to map it, ending up with a grayscale lighting stencil. There are many different ways to pre-generate Dark Maps. Radiosity baked into a texture and manual generation are the two most common methods.

Once you have a nice shadow map, use a new UVW channel to map it onto your object with a new UVW modifier. Load the shadow map through the Dark-Map load button in the Gamebryo .

When lights are not moving in a scene, do not use dynamic lighting if at all possible. You should use vertex colors or dark maps. Giving all your lights attenuation is important to help with this. If you find it easier to use dynamic lights to light your scene, your programmer may be able to process your scene by automatically burning in dynamic lights into the vertex colors for you. Check with your programmer if you need this option.
