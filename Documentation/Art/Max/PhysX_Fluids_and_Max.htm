PhysX Fluids and Max

Gamebryo supports the conversion of Gamebryo particle systems into PhysX fluid systems to be simulated on PhysX hardware. Any Max "PCloud" particle system can be converted into a fluid system. The rendering-related properties, such as particle properties, particle animations, shading etc. are all used to render the fluid particles. Any parameters related to the motion of the particles (gravity, wind fields, etc.) are all ignored.

Fluid Emitters

Fluid emitters are automatically created out of the PCloud particle system emitter. The emission rate, particle lifetime and emission velocity are all respected, but other emission properties are not.

All fluid emitters are planar polygonal shapes that lie in the local X-Y plane of the particle system emitter and emit in the positive Z direction. The shape, rectangular or elliptical, is based on the PCloud emitter shape (box, cylinder or sphere).

To attach an emitter to a PhysX actor, link it as a child of the actor in Max. The export process will automatically associate the emitter with the actor.

Exporting Fluids

The conversion to PhysX fluids is done by the PhysX Extractor tool plug-in when the "Convert to PhysX Fluids" options are checked. All of the other content in the scene is also prepared for use with fluids. Specifically:

  • All static physics-enabled objects are exported as mesh shapes, and all mesh shapes are exported as static actors, because this is the only supported arrangement for static geometry in a fluid simulation.
  • Convex shapes must have fewer then 32 faces when converted to convex geometry for hardware, so you must significantly reduce the number of subdivisions for spheres, capsules, etc that are non-uniformly scaled. If the fluid is intended to lie on the surface, then you must also use these low tessellations for the rendered shape, or the fluid will appear to move strangely over the surface.

If you plan on merging scenes into one simulation at run-time, these limitations on collision shapes must apply in all the scenes that will be merged.
