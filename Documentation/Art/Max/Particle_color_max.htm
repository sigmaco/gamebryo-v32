Particle color.max

The particle color.max sample uses the particle-color map slot to modulate the color and alpha of particles. In addition, it is an example of particles bouncing off a deflector gizmo and using gravity.

Particle color.max sample

This sample has a ground plane (Plane01) with a particle system (SuperSpray01) centered in the middle. SuperSpray01 has a gravity force (Gravity01) attached to it as well as a planar deflector (Deflector01) that exactly coincides with Plane01. Plane01 is shaded with the Floor material. The SuperSpray's particles are shaded by the Particle material.

Note that the alpha value that is used to fade out the particles is obtained by averaging the red, green, and blue components of the "Particle Age" material's color swatches. Additionally, the percent values of the "Particle Opacity" slot are ignored and only those from the "Particle Color" slot's "Particle Age" material are used. The "Particle Color" and "Particle Opacity" slots actually specify the vertex color of each particle as it ages. Use of vertex colors must be enabled for the "Particles" material.

This step is done by ensuring that the "Apply Mode" is set to MODULATE, that vertex colors are turned on EMISSIVE, and E is specified by the "Src Mode" and "Light Mode" fields.
