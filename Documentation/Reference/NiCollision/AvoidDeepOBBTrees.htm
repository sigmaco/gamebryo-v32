Avoid Deep OBB Trees

The circumstances are ideal for using OBB trees when the triangle count is relatively small and you're dealing with rigid bodies. An OBB tree depth of 4 or 5 will be optimal for most circumstances. Only rarely should larger depths be considered, since that level of detail on the collision is typically not needed.

For geometric objects with a large number of triangles, the OBB trees can be too deep to be of practical use. The problem with deeper trees is that the OBBs at the children nodes tend not to be much different than the OBB of the parent node. A lot of time is therefore spent on box-box intersection testing that always yields the same result. This result is a known consequence of bounding volume tree algorithms when the two objects are in close proximity.
