Collision and Non-NiMesh Objects

Objects in a scene graph that have no actual triangle-based geometry in their hierarchy will have NiBounds with zero radii and will therefore never return positive results in collision detection. If non-NiMesh objects are needed as part of a collision simulation, they either may have an "application-culled-for-rendering" triangle-primitive proxy mesh or may have an alternate bounding volume (ABV) for the object, in which case the collision callback must take care of the desired behavior for the application.

An example of this approach would be attaching ABVs to the bones of a skinned character. Typically, an application wants collision to affect the position of a character's bones, rather than the skin of the character directly. The bones' ABVs efficiently serve this purpose by acting as proxies for the collision system.

Of course, in some advanced cases it might be desirable to have the skin of the character deform because of a collision. In those situations, applications should be prepared for more expensive collision operations that will likely require triangle-accurate collision detection.
