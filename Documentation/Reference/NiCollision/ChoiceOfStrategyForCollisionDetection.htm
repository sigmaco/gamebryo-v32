Choice Of Strategy For Collision Detection

Because indiscriminate use of collision detection can be an expensive operation in a real-time 3D application, you'll want to use the right approach for the right circumstances.

There's a saying, "If all you have is a hammer, then every problem looks like a nail." There may be circumstances when a fully general black-box collision system may be needed. Alternatively, there may be cases where simpler alternatives work much better. Often, combinations of approaches may be needed, to get the most out of each situation.

Regardless of the collision detection approaches you choose, you'll want to prototype and profile your use of them first, to make sure they meet your needs for performance, collision detection behavior, etc.

The accompanying sections cover different collision detection approaches in the context of the different situations for which each might be most applicable.
