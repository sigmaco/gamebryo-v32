Portals Benefits

The basic benefit of the NiPortal library is that the cell-portal scene graph structure allows for large-scale, sprawling interiors that can be rendered at high performance. Portals are capable of culling huge amounts of data without even testing them for visibility. Without portals, large levels often result in extensive "overdraw"—multiple rooms being rendered to the screen on top of one another, even though only the nearest is visible. This slows both the geometric pipeline (which must transform and light this occluded data) and the rendering hardware (which must draw needless pixels on top of one another).

A portal system lowers the amount of overdraw by rendering the room containing the viewer and only the parts of any other rooms that can be seen through doorways, windows, etc. In this way, the portal system works by processing potentially visible geometry only, and avoids even accessing entire wings or floors of buildings that cannot be seen.
