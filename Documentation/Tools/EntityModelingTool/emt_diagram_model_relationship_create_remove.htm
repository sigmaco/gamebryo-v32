Creating Relationships Between Models and Presets in a Diagram

Relationships between models determine how properties and behaviors are passed from one model to another. When creating relationships, you always have a source model and a target model. The direction of the link always begins at the source and ends at the target. The source is the parent model, which means the properties and behaviors of the source are passed down and mixed with those of the target or child model. Within a single diagram, you can experiment with different relationships until you find the right mix.

Note

Each model within a diagram can have several parent and child models.

To create relationships between models and presets within a diagram

  1. Load a diagram into the Diagram Editor.
  2. Mouse over the source or parent model.

    The mix-in arrow is displayed.

  3. Drag the mix-in arrow to the target or child model.

    A relationship is created between the source model and the target model whereby all the properties and behaviors of the source are mixed with those of the target.

To remove a relationship between two models/presets:

  1. Load a diagram into the Diagram Editor.
  2. Right click the mix-in arrow that links two models and select Delete from the menu.

    The relationship between the two models is broken, which means that the properties and behaviors of the parent model are no longer mixed with those of the child model.
