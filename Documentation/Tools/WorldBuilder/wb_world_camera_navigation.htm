Navigating Around the World

Navigating the world in World Builder can be performed using the mouse or keyboard. With an assortment of handy camera modes and different ways of warping around the world.

Keyboard Navigation

Keyboard navigation is consistent across any of the camera modes.

  • [W] moves the camera forward.
  • [A] moves the camera backward.
  • [S] moves the camera left.
  • [D] moves the camera right.
  • [Shift] enters turbo mode, while the key is held down camera translations using the mouse or keyboard will move at a faster rate.

Mouse Navigation

Mouse interaction changes within World Builder depending upon which Camera Mode you are using. Below are the standard camera mode controls.

Left Mouse Button

  • [click] select / deselect an object.
  • [click & hold] rotate camera (look around the world).
  • [+ Ctrl] multi-select (click again on other entities to add/remove them from the selection).
  • [+ Alt] marquee-select (select a group of entities in one motion).
  • [+ Shift] clone the selected entities using the Translate, Rotate, or Scale gizmo.

Right Mouse Button

  • [click] context menu.
  • [click & hold] drive camera (move around the world).

Left + Right Mouse Button

  • [click & hold] elevator camera (moves the camera up and down and left and right).

Middle Mouse Button

  • [click & hold] pans the camera.

Scroll Wheel

  • [up] zoom in.
  • [down] zoom out.

To adjust the sensitivity and speed of these controls there are settings available in Utilities > Settings > [User] > World Builder > Camera.

Camera Modes

The camera control modes and camera tools are bundled together and are found along with the other tool icons.

Camera modes are selected by toggling between 1 of the 3 camera navigation modes. Each has a different navigational affect on the viewport cameras when the mouse is used. Across all modes, the operation of the middle mouse wheel behaves the same. Scrolling the mouse wheel will move the camera forward and back along the depth axis.

Standard

When in standard mode (), clicking the left mouse button and dragging will rotate the camera. This effectively gives the ability to look around the world.

Orbit

When in orbit mode (), if an entity is not selected, the orbit point will be determined by casting a ray from the center of the viewport into the scene. Whatever entity is hit first becomes the orbit point. If an entity is already selected or becomes selected, then that entity is used as the orbit point. The actual orbiting action is performed by clicking the left mouse button and dragging in the viewport.

Pan

When in Pan mode (), navigation occurs soley along the viewport plane. Click the left mouse button and dragging in the viewport will move the camera along one or both of the two axis depending on the direction of the drag.

Camera Tools

Fly Level

When the "Toggle Fly Level" button () is pressed, the camera can be moved using the WASD keys but will stay locked in the same horizontal plane. Press the icon again to turn this feature off.

Look At Selection

Pressing the "Look At Selection" button () while having an entity selected will cause the camera to rotate toward the selected entity. The cameras translation, however, will be preserved.

Move to Selection

Pressing the "Move to Selection" button () while having one or more entities selected will perform a zoom to extents on the selection.

Miscellaneous Navigation

The user can also double click on an entity in the Entity View to perform a "Move to Selection" on that entity.
