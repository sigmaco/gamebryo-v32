Render View

You can use the Render View in the Animation Tool to view, play, and interact with animations.

To display this view, select View > Render View or click the Render View button on the main toolbar.

Render View in the Animation Tool

Three modes enable you to manipulate the scene in different ways:

  • Orbit mode—Orbits the camera around the character model. When you click and drag the left mouse button, the camera will orbit up, down, left, and right around the character about its up-axis. You can zoom in or out by scrolling the mouse wheel, or you can drag up and down while pressing down the Shift key. If you drag while pressing down the Control or Alt key, you can constrain the orbiting to either up and down or left and right, respectively. By clicking the middle mouse button and dragging, you can specify the point on the character model around which the camera orbits. You can reset this point by clicking the Reset Orbit Point button in the toolbar.
  • Pan mode—Enables you to control the camera independent from the character model. By clicking the left mouse button and dragging, you can pan the camera's orbit point up, down, left, and right. When you scroll the mouse wheel, you can translate forward and backward. Dragging up and down while holding down the Shift key will also translate forwards and backwards. If you drag while pressing down the Control or Alt key, you can constrain the camera to either up and down or left and right, respectively.
  • Scene Height mode— Provides control over the height of the background scene relative to the character model. By clicking the left mouse button and dragging up and down, you can adjust the height of the background scene. This height is saved out with each project.

Additionally, right-clicking in the Render View will bring up a context menu that enables you to issue commands, adjust the scene options, or change the renderer background color.
